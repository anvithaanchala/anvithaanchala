seeing the world move — optical flow DATMO

detection and tracking of moving objects for autonomous vehicles

i wanted to know if a car could understand a chaotic intersection the way a human driver does, not just detecting objects but predicting where they're going. built a full detection and tracking pipeline using LiDAR point clouds, DBSCAN clustering, and extended kalman filters running in real time. then i stress-tested it against published IEEE benchmarks inside CARLA simulation, because trusting your own system blindly felt wrong. spoiler: the published numbers weren't always right.

PythonROS2LiDARDBSCANEKFCARLA
🦾

a robot that feeds you — assistive feeding arm

ai-powered robot for people with motor impairments

this one is personal. i wanted to build something that gives back independence, a robotic arm that watches your face, waits for you to open your mouth, and delivers a bite of food. sounds simple. it really isn't. paired a WidowX 250S arm with an Intel RealSense camera, wired MediaPipe Face Mesh to detect mouth-open in real time, and built the whole pipeline in ROS2 with MoveIt handling motion planning. 90% task success rate. under $5K. most meaningful thing i've built.

ROS2MoveItMediaPipeRealSenseWidowX 250SOpenCV
⛸️

computer vision on ice — figure skating analysis

real-time athlete tracking and pose estimation, deployed to ios

what if you could break down a triple axel frame by frame? built an end-to-end CV pipeline for athlete motion analysis on live figure skating footage. RT-DETR for detection, ByteTrack across occlusion-heavy ice rink scenes, RTMPose for biomechanical keypoints, and a custom ResNet-34 to segment the ice surface itself. ships to coaches on their phones via cloud inference.

RT-DETRByteTrackRTMPoseResNet-34PyTorchiOS
💜

a pillow that listens — pillavate

smart assistive device for CHF patients

congestive heart failure patients often wake in the night struggling to breathe, and the right pillow angle can genuinely help. pillavate monitors SpO2 and heart rate via pulse oximetry and automatically adjusts elevation using a servo motor. built on Arduino. small thing, real impact.

ArduinoPulse OximetryServo ControlEmbedded C
